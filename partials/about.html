About

I write JavaScript, CSS, HTML, and PHP. I used to work with Java, C, and C++, but not lately.

Framework-wise, I've worked with jQuery, ExtJS, Joomla, and Lumen on the job. I use React/Redux when working on personal projects. I used to run this site on the typical Jekyll setup for Github pages, but I recently rewrote the whole thing in Angular 1.4. I didn't see much point in learning Angular 1 with version 2 and its many changes looming, but according to recruiters people who do not have intimate knowledge of the field, if I don't know Angular, my years of JavaScript experience mean nothing.

I have a B.S. and M.S. in Computer Science from Stevens Institute of Technology. Most of my development work there was done in C under a UNIX-like environment. My favorite project was implementing a full HTTP/1.0 web server.

At present I work as a 50/50 (well, more like 80/20) front-end/PHP developer at a medium-sized marketing company. My tasks generally involve:

  • Building of microservices (usually backed by Lumen) to centralize common functionality across separate properties,
  • JS/CSS/HTML development for said microservices when a frontend is required,
  • Integration of modern UI build processes (Sass/Gulp/Webpack) to an otherwise aging codebase.

Other interests include music, good beer, and baseball. I am lifelong fan of the New York Mets (even after they choked harder than I've seen in a long time), and would appreciate nothing more than a World Series win during my lifetime. While I have not made much progress yet, I also plan to visit every team's home stadium at some point.
